Why WealthDelay Exists

Most people know their daily coffee costs $5. Very few know it costs over $150,000 over a 30-year career when you account for compounding investment returns. Not because people are bad with money — but because nobody shows them the full picture.

The financial industry profits when you carry balances, hold cash, and delay investing. Subscription services count on autopay inertia. The entire consumer economy is designed to make spending feel smaller than it is, and to make the future feel abstract and distant.

WealthDelay is the counterweight. Every tool on this site takes a simple financial input and shows you the compounded, inflation-adjusted, retirement-impacted truth of what that decision actually costs you over your lifetime. We believe financial clarity is not a luxury — it should be freely available to everyone.

Our Methodology

Every WealthDelay calculator runs entirely in your browser using verified financial formulas — the same mathematics used by certified financial planners and taught in finance textbooks. No data is ever sent to a server.

We use four core frameworks across our tools:

  • Future Value of an Annuity — calculates what recurring spending would grow to if invested instead, using the formula FV = PMT × [((1+r)^n − 1) / r], where r is the monthly return and n is the number of months.
  • Inflation adjustment — converts nominal future values to real purchasing power. At 3% inflation, $500,000 in 30 years is worth approximately $206,000 in today's money.
  • Retirement delay calculation — translates opportunity cost into months of retirement delayed, based on a standard safe withdrawal rate applied to your current savings trajectory.
  • Life-hours metric — converts financial decisions into irreplaceable time at your hourly wage, making abstract dollar figures personal and immediate.

We use 7% as the default annual investment return, representing the S&P 500's inflation-adjusted historical average over the past century. Users can adjust this assumption in most tools. All calculations are estimates based on historical averages and are not guarantees of future performance.

The 30 Tools

WealthDelay covers five major categories of personal finance, with tools designed to make each category's true cost visible and actionable.

  • Opportunity cost tools — Coffee, streaming, eating out, smoking, gym membership, and other daily spending habits converted to their lifetime investment equivalent.
  • Debt cost tools — Credit card true cost, mortgage overpayment, student loan payoff, car loan total cost, Buy Now Pay Later impact, and debt payoff strategy comparison.
  • Income tools — Salary negotiation lifetime value, true hourly rate after expenses, side hustle investment equivalent, and 401(k) contribution impact.
  • Retirement tools — FIRE number calculator, inflation vs savings erosion, how long savings will last, retirement wealth estimator, and net worth target timeline.
  • Lifestyle tools — Lifestyle inflation, true cost of a bigger house, luxury car opportunity cost, rent vs buy, index funds vs stock picking, and emergency fund sizing.

Who Uses WealthDelay

WealthDelay is used by people at every stage of their financial journey. Our most common users are people in their 20s and 30s who are beginning to think seriously about building wealth, people carrying debt who want to understand the true cost of that debt, and people approaching major life decisions like buying a house, changing jobs, or planning for early retirement.

We also see use from financial educators, personal finance writers, and people who want a transparent second opinion on a financial decision they are already considering. Because our tools are fully transparent about their formulas and assumptions, they are useful as both teaching tools and personal decision-support tools.

Our audience is primarily based in the United States and Canada, though the underlying financial mathematics applies to anyone making long-term financial decisions.

Our Principles

  • Free forever. Financial clarity should not be a premium feature. All 30 calculators are and will remain free to use without any account or payment required.
  • No data stored. Your financial situation is private. Every calculation runs in your browser. We never see your inputs, we never store them, and they are never transmitted anywhere.
  • Honest math. We use standard financial formulas and disclose all assumptions. We show both nominal and inflation-adjusted values so you see the complete picture.
  • No dark patterns. We do not hide results behind email walls, require signups to see your numbers, or design calculators to produce misleading results.
  • Transparent about limitations. Our tools are planning aids, not financial plans. We consistently recommend consulting a qualified financial advisor for major decisions.

Revenue Model

WealthDelay is free and supported by display advertising through Google AdSense, and clearly marked affiliate links to financial products we believe are genuinely useful to our audience. We never accept payment to feature a product prominently or to influence calculator results.

We also offer optional paid PDF reports for users who want a formatted, downloadable version of their calculator results. The full calculator results are always free on screen — the PDF is a convenience option only.
