Common Questions

How is opportunity cost calculated?
+
We use the Future Value of an Annuity formula: FV = PMT √ó [((1+r)^n ‚àí 1) / r] where PMT is your monthly equivalent spending, r is the monthly return rate, and n is months until retirement. This is what your money would compound to if invested in a broad market index instead.
What does inflation adjustment mean?
+
Nominal future values overstate real purchasing power. We adjust with Real Value = Nominal √∑ (1+inflation)^years. At 3% inflation, $500,000 in 30 years is worth about $206,000 in today's dollars. We show both so you see the complete picture.
Is a 7% return realistic?
+
The S&P 500 has returned approximately 10% nominally and 7% inflation-adjusted annually over the past century. 7% is the standard assumption in financial planning. Individual results vary based on timing and allocation. This is a planning tool, not a guarantee.
Does this tool store my data?
+
No. Every calculation runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server. No cookies, no tracking of your inputs. You can verify this by using the tool with your internet connection turned off.
What is the life-hours metric?
+
We convert the total opportunity cost into hours of work at your wage: Hours = Opportunity Cost √∑ Hourly Wage. Every spending decision is ultimately a trade of your irreplaceable time. This makes abstract future numbers personal and concrete.
Should I cut all spending?
+
This tool creates awareness, not rules. Some spending has genuine value ‚Äî relationships, health, experiences. The goal is informed decisions: knowing exactly what something truly costs so you can consciously decide if it's worth it.
