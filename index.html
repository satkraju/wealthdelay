Common Questions

How is opportunity cost calculated?
+
We use the Future Value of an Annuity: FV = PMT √ó [((1+r)^n ‚àí 1) / r] where PMT is your monthly equivalent spending, r is the monthly return, and n is months to retirement. This is what your money compounds to if invested in a broad market index instead.
What does inflation adjustment mean?
+
Nominal future values overstate real purchasing power. We apply Real = Nominal √∑ (1+inflation)^years. At 3% inflation, $500K in 30 years is worth ~$206K today. We show both so you see the complete picture.
Is a 7% return realistic?
+
The S&P 500 has returned ~10% nominal and ~7% inflation-adjusted annually over the past century. 7% is the standard financial planning assumption. Individual results vary. This is a planning tool, not a guarantee.
Does this store my data?
+
No. All calculations run entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server. No cookies, no tracking of inputs. You can verify this by using the tool offline.
What is the life-hours metric?
+
We convert opportunity cost into hours of work at your wage: Hours = Opportunity Cost √∑ Hourly Wage. Every financial decision is ultimately a trade of your irreplaceable time. This makes abstract numbers personal.
Should I eliminate all spending?
+
This tool creates awareness, not rules. Some spending has genuine value. The goal is informed decisions ‚Äî knowing exactly what something truly costs so you can consciously decide if it's worth it to you.
